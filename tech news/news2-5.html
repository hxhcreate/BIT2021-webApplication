China's first offshore carbon dioxide storage demonstration project started

Editor: Chen Shiwen editor in charge: Liu Liang

Science and technology daily, Beijing, August 30 (reporter Qu Jian) - CNOOC announced on the 30th that China's first offshore carbon dioxide storage demonstration project was officially launched in the Pearl River Mouth Basin in the South China Sea, permanently storing the carbon dioxide associated with offshore oilfield development in an 800 meter deep seabed reservoir, with an annual storage of about 300000 tons and a total of more than 1.46 million tons. This is an important step in the green and low-carbon transformation of China's marine oil and gas development, and explores a new way for the realization of the national goal of "carbon peak and carbon neutralization".

Zhang Wei, deputy general manager and chief engineer of CNOOC Shenzhen Branch, introduced that the demonstration project is located in the Pearl River Estuary basin, about 190 kilometers southeast of Hong Kong, with an average water depth of more than 80 meters. It is an environmental protection supporting project for the development of Enping 15-1 oilfield group“ After the implementation of the project, it is equivalent to planting nearly 14 million trees or stopping nearly 1 million cars. "

He said that underground carbon dioxide storage is one of the effective measures to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Enping 15-1 Oilfield Group is the first oilfield group with high carbon dioxide content in the South China Sea. If it is developed according to the conventional mode, carbon dioxide will be produced out of the ground together with crude oil. Since this year, CNOOC has carried out research on Key Technologies of geological reservoir, drilling and completion and engineering integration suitable for offshore carbon dioxide storage, and successfully developed a complete set of technologies and equipment systems for carbon dioxide capture, treatment, injection, storage and monitoring of offshore platforms, filling the gap of China's offshore carbon dioxide storage technology.

"The carbon dioxide associated with the development of oil fields will be sealed in the salt water layer at a depth of 800 meters." Zhang Wei explained that the salt water layer has a dome structure and is covered with a thick argillaceous protective layer“ The injected carbon dioxide is sealed under the dome, which can effectively prevent gas overflow. "

It is reported that previously, domestic carbon dioxide storage field tests were concentrated in a few onshore areas. The successful implementation of the carbon dioxide storage project of Enping 15-1 Oilfield Group will open up new industries and formats of carbon dioxide storage in China, which is of great demonstration significance for the green development of offshore oil and gas fields.
