Fantasy-CyberDog

Synopsis


This robot that looks like a dog is actually a new product form that has attracted much attention in the industry - bionic quadruped robot.

Cyberdog has 11 groups of high-precision sensors on standby, which can actively detect external subtle changes. It is equipped with the AI supercomputer NVIDIA Jetson Xavier NX platform, which can process massive data from multiple sensors.

At the same time, cyberdog uses a self-developed high-performance servo motor to control the whole body motion module through Quanzhi mr813 chip.

CyberDog is a new ominous-looking robot from Xiaomi

Chinese tech giant Xiaomi has unveiled a quadrupedal robot named CyberDog: an experimental, open-source machine that the firm says “holds unforetold possibilities.” CyberDog is the latest example of tech companies embracing the quadrupedal form factor in robotics. The most notable example of the trend is Spot, a machine built by US firm Boston Dynamics. Spot went on sale last year for $74,500 and has been put to a range of uses, from surveying dangerous mines to helping doctors connect with patients remotely. It’s also been tested by both law enforcement and the military, though not as a weapon.

For life

On the one hand, bionic robots have great market potential in many fields such as service, engineering, security, medical treatment and so on.

On the other hand, as a global technology company, it is necessary for Xiaomi to make layout in cutting-edge fields as soon as possible and consolidate patent reserves.

Tech hgihlights

Powering CyberDog’s brain is the NVIDIA® Jetson Xavier™ NX platform, an AI Supercomputer for Embedded and Edge Systems which includes 384 CUDA® Cores, 48 Tensor Cores, a 6 Carmel ARM CPU, and 2 deep learning acceleration engines. For its owners, CyberDog processes massive data captured from its sensor system without a hitch.
